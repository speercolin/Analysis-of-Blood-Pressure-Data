Systolic Blood Pressure vs Alcohol Consumption

The graph to the left visualizes the relationship between the average systolic blood pressure in mmHg and alcohol consumption per capita in liters in the United States from 2000 to 2015.

Using the red linear regression as a guide, a strong, postive correlation can be seen between the two variables. This scatterplot suggests that there is a correlation between an individual's systolic blood pressure and the amount of alcohol they drink.
